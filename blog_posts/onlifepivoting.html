You want to switch career, start a business or get a promotion.

You want to lose weight, sleep longer, stop smoking or gain muscles.

You want to breakup, find a life partner, meet more girls or like-minded guys.

You want to buy a nice house, a dream car, a shiny watch or head-turning outfits.

You want to be less materialistic, live simply or be contempt with what you have.

You want to feel happier, complete, successful or more connected to meaningful others.

You want to be sociable, emotionally intelligent, less needy or more balanced.

You want to be able to have difficult conversations, display honesty toward yourself and others in all situations.

You want. Wanting invites either stagnation or unhabitual change.

Stagnation implies pain.

Unhabitual change implies pain.

Stagnation implies an unconscious, visceral, long-lasting, deferred, potentially explosive pain leading to the absolute certainty of an unfulfilled want.

Unhabitual change implies a conscious, acute, ephemeral, likely mind-expanding pain leading to the potential fulfillment of your want.

Being conscious of these two types of pains and their outcomes makes the choice between stagnation and unhabitual change obvious.

Through courage to endure pain of unhabitual change, living your best life becomes more likely.
