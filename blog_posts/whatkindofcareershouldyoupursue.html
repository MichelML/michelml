If you google this question online, you will find that this question has been answered multiple times.

I bet many people will provide you with specific answers with a detailed explanation on how these professions fit your personality, skills, temperament. And this is fine – being conscious of your human nature is a good thing, and hopefully it will guide you toward a career you will enjoy.

There is one thing though - nobody will nor can decide for you in the end. A better approach at rapidly finding a career you enjoy is the following:

  • Learn broadly and constantly about many topics to form a deep view of the world you live in: psychology, philosophy, politics, engineering, mathematics, physics, arts, history, etc.
  • Find what problems are important to you and the people around you as your learning about the world progresses.
  • Determine the skills needed to fix these problems and acquire them.
  • Connect with people interested in solving the same problems you want to solve.
  • With your courage, patience, and self-directedness, be willing to try and actually fix the problems you care about, and try to surround yourself with critical but empowering influences wanting you to succeed.

Self-directedness toward fixing the problems that matter to you with people sharing your view is your only path to having a career that will be meaningful to you in the end.

Don't waste too much time listening to other people's opinions. In the matter of career choices, do your own thinking.
