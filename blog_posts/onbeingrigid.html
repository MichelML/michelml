The personality traits that are usually harmful are the ones that are too rigid.

By rigid, I mean traits you display in multiple scenarios as an unconscious defense mechanism, that usually result in lasting and repeating negative consequences for yourself or your relationships.

Jeffrey E. Young, a clinical psychologist, professor at Columbia University, and inventor of Schema Therapy and the term lifetraps, wrote extensively about these rigid traits, namely in his book Reinventing Your Life, in which he describes the origin of these type of traits, the way they manifest, and how to have better control over these manifestations.

If you want to better understand your human nurture, how your past influences your present personality, digging into Jeffrey E. Young’s writings is a very good starting point.
