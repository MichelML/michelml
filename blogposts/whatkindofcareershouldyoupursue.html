If you google this question online, you will find this question has been answered multiple times.

I would bet many people provide specific answers based on personality, skills, temperament. And this is fine – being conscious of your human nature is a good thing, and hopefully it will guide you toward a career you will enjoy.

However, nobody will nor can choose for you in the end. Here is an approach that will lead you to choose a career that adds meaning to your life:

  • Learn broadly and constantly on many topics. Psychology, philosophy, politics, engineering, mathematics, physics, arts, history, etc. It will help you form a broad view of the world you live in.
  • Find the problems that are important to you and the people around you. As your view of the world expands, do not hesitate to change course along the way.
  • Determine the skills needed to fix the problems you want to solve and acquire them. Persevere.
  • Connect with people interested in solving the same problems you want to solve.
  • With courage, patience, and self-directedness, be willing to try and fix the identified problems. Again, try to surround yourself with critical but empowering influences wanting you to succeed.

Self-directedness toward fixing the problems that matter to you, with the right people besides you, is your only path to having a meaningful career.

Don't waste too much time listening to other people's opinions. In the matter of career choices, do your own thinking and experiencing.
