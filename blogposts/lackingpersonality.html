Lacking personality can mean a lot of things.

However, in my humble opinion, a common usage of the term lack of personality that hinders one’s own well-being over the long term relates to personality repression.

Personality repression means excessively complying and living one’s life through the influence of other people’s desires/opinions, ignoring one’s own. Not having a sense of self-expression and self-directedness for one’s own life, always referring to what other people are doing to figure out what to do next for oneself.

As a real-life example of that, you can imagine someone who radically changes her day-to-day behaviors every time she changes her life partner, whether it be a boyfriend, girlfriend, etc.

Other real-life potential cues of personality repression:

  • A ridiculously high frequency of “me too” or similar statements in someone’s conversation stance with another person.
  • Drastic personality modification depending on the group a person spends time with. It can be in the way someone dresses, talks, thinks, eats, exercises, or any other manifestation of personality.
  • A recurrent feeling of unease after saying yes to someone, when you really wanted to say no (or vice versa).

Jeffrey Young talks about this concept in his book Reinventing Your Life, in which he discusses different lifetraps people can fall into that are usually developed through emotionally-charged past life experiences. The one that relates to personality repression in his book is called the subjugation lifetrap, as in subjugating to other people’s needs and wants.

I highly recommend his book if the concept of personality repression resonated with you, even if to a mild extent.

Take good care.
